About Me

I am an artist native to Santa Fe, New Mexico. A culuter that desires Art, Turquoise, and green chile. It is an inspiring and spicy place with purple sunsets and bright orange sunrises. It allows for the imagination to play, grow, and adventure into the unseen, overlooked, and forgotten. A simple charcoal drawing of a bell pepper can be tranquil when the eye is forced to see the prespective of the artist. Is the bell pepper red? Is it green? How can one drawing be so different from person to person? The beauty of art is on the eye of the beholder as well as the answers. The artisit job is to give you the questions.

Wood Art

My wood art is my newest addition to styles of art. The smell of the wood buring is claming. Reminds me of my grandpa smoking his tabaco pipe, sweet and smokey. The variations of wood art are exciting. From wood burning, painting on wood, or carving wooden scupltures.

Charcoal Art

My charcoal paintings helped me understand different shades of black and white. How it is not all black and white, but grays and variations of light and darkness. Reminds me that things are not always how they seem. I also like black and white paintings because it allows the viewer to imagin what the image looks like in color. Can someone tell if the bell pepper is red or green by just it's looks? Maybe...

Acrylic Art

My Acrylic paintins are my oldest form of style. I find this type to be most challenging. As I feel I am great with shading in black and white. Color brings a whole new concept to shading. Muliple colors can go into shading the color red. From the light side to the dark side. Still a challenge I have yet to accomplish.
